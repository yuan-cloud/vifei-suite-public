# Business Model v1

## Objective

Turn Vifei from strong OSS proof tooling into a durable product with clear enterprise value.

## Product tiers (proposed)

1. OSS Core
- Local-first deterministic run evidence cockpit.
- CLI, TUI, replay artifacts, and share-safe refusal flow.

2. Team Edition (future)
- Multi-user collaboration features.
- Managed policy packs and stronger operational reporting.
- Hosted update channel and support.

3. Enterprise Edition (future)
- SSO, RBAC, policy governance, and compliance-oriented controls.
- Deployment support, SLA-backed support, and audit workflow integrations.

## ICP (initial)

1. AI-native startups with production agent workflows.
2. Mid-market teams with security and governance requirements.
3. Enterprise platform groups standardizing agent reliability posture.

## Pricing posture (initial hypothesis)

1. OSS remains free and open.
2. Team edition: seat-based plus usage limits.
3. Enterprise: annual contracts with support and governance add-ons.

## Revenue levers

1. Hosted control-plane and operational dashboards.
2. Compliance and policy management modules.
3. Enterprise onboarding and integration services.
4. Premium support and incident-response advisory.

## Product-to-revenue bridge

1. OSS acquisition via deterministic trust story.
2. Team adoption via easier collaboration and policy operations.
3. Enterprise conversion via governance, security, and support guarantees.

## Key risks

1. Long OSS-to-paid conversion cycle.
2. Competing observability platforms adding partial replay features.
3. Under-scoped onboarding can limit adoption despite strong core capability.

## Mitigations

1. Keep proof-first differentiation clear in all messaging.
2. Ship polished onboarding and showcase experience.
3. Prioritize enterprise-grade controls that map to real procurement needs.
