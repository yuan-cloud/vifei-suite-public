# Panopticon Founder One-Pager

## What we are building

Panopticon is a deterministic, local-first evidence cockpit for AI agent runs. It records canonical run truth, replays it reliably, and produces proof artifacts teams can verify directly.

## Why now

Agent adoption is accelerating; teams have visibility tools, but they still struggle to prove replay consistency and safe sharing under pressure. Trust gaps become incident, governance, and procurement blockers.

## The core wedge

1. Canonical ordering via `commit_index`.
2. Deterministic replay and stable proof artifacts.
3. Honest overload posture where truth remains intact and projection degrades.
4. Share-safe export refusal with explicit blocked-field reporting.

## Who this is for

1. AI platform teams running agent workflows.
2. Security and governance teams auditing run behavior.
3. Reliability and incident teams debugging regressions.

## Why we win

Most tools optimize for observability convenience; Panopticon optimizes for deterministic evidence. That distinction matters when teams need to verify, compare, and safely share run outcomes.

## Product surface today

1. CLI and TUI workflows for import, replay, tour, and export.
2. Deterministic artifact set (`metrics.json`, `viewmodel.hash`, captures).
3. Showcase profile and presentation page for public product storytelling.

## Product surface next

1. Premium showcase demos that highlight determinism and safety in motion.
2. Polished launch assets and static showcase publishing lane.
3. Packaging and onboarding quality improvements for faster adoption.

## Business path

1. OSS trust acquisition through verifiable demos.
2. Team workflow expansion through collaboration and policy features.
3. Enterprise conversion through governance controls, support, and integration.

## Near-term success signals

1. Users can run one command and verify hash stability on rerun.
2. Public launch content clearly differentiates Panopticon from generic tracing tools.
3. Early adopters use refusal-safe export as part of real workflows.
